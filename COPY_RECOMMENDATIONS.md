# SafeVoice Copy Audit & Recommendations
## Domain Expert Analysis: Trauma-Informed GBV/FGM Reporting

---

## 🎯 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Current Issues:**
1. **Generic safety language** - Doesn't address specific fears of FGM/GBV victims
2. **Missing trauma-informed principles** - Copy assumes rational decision-making
3. **No cultural sensitivity** - Ignores shame, family pressure, community stigma
4. **Weak CTAs** - Doesn't address "Why should I report?" or "What happens next?"
5. **Report page lacks context** - No reassurance about FGM-specific support

**Key Principle:** Victims need to feel **BELIEVED, SAFE, and IN CONTROL** at every step.

---

## 📱 PAGE-BY-PAGE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 1. LANDING PAGE

#### ❌ CURRENT:
```
"Report FGM and abuse anonymously. Get help immediately. Your voice matters."
```

#### ✅ RECOMMENDED:
```
"What happened to you is not your fault. Report safely. Get help. Stay anonymous."
```

**Why:** 
- Leads with validation (trauma-informed)
- "Not your fault" addresses shame/guilt
- "Stay anonymous" = control + safety

---

### 2. DASHBOARD (Incident Selection)

#### ❌ CURRENT:
```
"Choose what happened:"

- Violence at home
- Harm to a child
- Unwanted touching or harassment
- Other safety concern
```

**MISSING:** FGM is not listed! This is critical.

#### ✅ RECOMMENDED:

**Header:**
```
"What do you need help with? (You can report without giving your name)"
```

**Incident Types:**
```
- Female Genital Cutting (FGM/C) - Someone cut me or wants to cut me
- Violence at home - Someone hurt me or threatens me
- Sexual abuse - Someone touched me without permission
- Child in danger - A child is being hurt
- Forced marriage - I'm being forced to marry
- Other harm - Something else happened
```

**Why:**
- "Female Genital Cutting" is more culturally sensitive than "FGM"
- Descriptive subtitles help victims identify their situation
- "Someone cut me or wants to cut me" = immediate + preventive
- "Forced marriage" is common co-occurrence with FGM
- Reminder about anonymity reduces fear

---

### 3. REPORT PAGE

#### ❌ CURRENT ISSUES:

**Title:** "Submit Report" (bureaucratic, cold)

**Description prompt:** "Tell us what happened in your own words..."
- Too open-ended for traumatized victims
- No guidance on what's helpful to share

**Anonymous notice:** "Your report is completely anonymous unless you choose to provide contact information below."
- Buried at top
- Doesn't explain HOW anonymity works

#### ✅ RECOMMENDED CHANGES:

**Title:**
```
"Tell Your Story Safely"
```

**Anonymous Notice (Make it prominent):**
```
🔒 This report is completely anonymous
• We don't track your phone or location
• You don't have to give your name
• Only share what feels safe
• You can stop anytime
```

**Description Section:**

**Current:** "Tell us what happened *"

**New:**
```
"What happened? (Share only what you're comfortable with)"

Placeholder: "You can write as much or as little as you want. There's no right or wrong way to tell your story."

Help text below:
"It helps if you can share:
• What happened (you don't need all the details)
• When it happened (even just 'last week' or 'a long time ago')
• Who did it (if you know them)
• If you're still in danger

You don't have to answer everything. Share what feels safe."
```

**Why:**
- Removes pressure ("only what you're comfortable with")
- Structured prompts help victims organize thoughts
- "If you're still in danger" = triage for urgent cases
- Validates incomplete reports

---

**Category Selection:**

**Current:** "Help us categorize this (Optional)"

**New (if FGM selected from dashboard):**
```
"Tell us more about the cutting (Optional but helpful)"

Options:
- It already happened to me
- Someone is planning to cut me
- Someone is planning to cut a child I know
- I was cut as a child and need help now
- I don't want to say
```

**Why:**
- Specific to FGM context
- Helps triage urgency (prevention vs. post-trauma care)
- "I don't want to say" = respects boundaries

---

**Contact Information Section:**

**Current:** "Include contact information for follow-up"

**New:**
```
"Do you want someone to contact you? (Completely optional)"

⚠️ Safety First:
Only give your contact info if it's SAFE for someone to reach you.

If someone checks your phone or email, it might not be safe to be contacted.

You can still get help without giving contact information.
```

**Contact Method Options - ADD:**
```
- Email
- Phone call
- WhatsApp
- Text/SMS
- I'll check back on this app (no contact needed)
```

**Best Time to Contact - REWRITE:**

**Current:** "When is it safe to contact you?"

**New:**
```
"When is it SAFE for us to contact you?"

- Morning (8AM-12PM) - when I'm alone
- Afternoon (12PM-6PM) - when I'm at work/school  
- Evening (6PM-10PM) - when family is asleep
- Only contact if I reach out first
```

**Why:**
- Emphasizes SAFETY not convenience
- Context clues help victims think through risk
- "Only contact if I reach out first" = ultimate control

---

**Submit Button:**

**Current:** "Submit Report"

**New:**
```
"Send My Report Safely"
```

**Accuracy Notice - REMOVE OR REWRITE:**

**Current:** "By submitting this report, you acknowledge that the information provided is accurate to the best of your knowledge."

**Problem:** Legal language scares victims. Sounds like they'll get in trouble for mistakes.

**New (if you must keep it):**
```
"We know memory can be unclear after trauma. Just tell us what you remember - that's enough."
```

### 4. CONFIRMATION PAGE

#### ❌ CURRENT:
```
"Thank You - Your report has been sent safely"
"We will look at your report within 24 hours. You are brave for speaking up."
```

**Issues:**
- "Thank you" feels transactional
- "You are brave" can feel patronizing
- Doesn't address immediate safety

#### ✅ RECOMMENDED:

```
"Your Report Was Received"

✓ Your report is safe with us
✓ We will review it within 24 hours
✓ You did the right thing by speaking up

Are you safe right now?

[Button: I need help now] [Button: I'm safe for now]
```

**What Next Section - REWRITE:**

**Current:** "What do you want to do next?"

**New:**
```
"What do you need right now?"

[Card: Talk to someone now]
"Chat with a trained counselor (anonymous)"

[Card: Find help near me]
"Hospitals, shelters, and support centers in your area"

[Card: Learn about my rights]
"What the law says about FGM and abuse in Nigeria"

[Card: Safety planning]
"How to stay safe if you're still in danger"

[Card: Report something else]
"Tell us about another incident"
```

**Why:**
- Focuses on victim's immediate needs
- "Learn about my rights" = empowerment
- "Safety planning" = practical harm reduction
- More actionable than generic "find help nearby"

---

### 5. EMERGENCY HELP (Dashboard)

#### ❌ CURRENT:
```
"Need Help Right Now?"
[Button: Get Help Now]
```

**Issue:** Doesn't explain what "help" means. Is it police? Counselor? Ambulance?

#### ✅ RECOMMENDED:
```
"Are You in Danger Right Now?"

If someone is hurting you or you're bleeding, you need help immediately.

[Button: Connect Me to Help Now]

This will:
• Connect you to a trained counselor
• Help you call emergency services if needed
• Give you safety planning support

This is NOT the police (unless you want us to call them).
```

**Why:**
- Clarifies what "help" means
- Addresses fear of police involvement
- Specific about physical danger (bleeding = FGM complication)

## 🎨 TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

### DO:
✅ Use **simple, direct language** (6th-grade reading level)
✅ Lead with **safety and control** ("You choose," "Only if safe")
✅ Validate feelings ("What happened is not your fault")
✅ Be specific about what happens next (no vague promises)
✅ Use "you" and "your" (personal, not institutional)
✅ Acknowledge cultural context (shame, family, community)

### DON'T:
❌ Use legal/medical jargon ("perpetrator," "incident," "victim")
❌ Make assumptions ("You are brave" - they might not feel brave)
❌ Use passive voice ("Your report will be reviewed" → "We will review")
❌ Overpromise ("We'll keep you safe" - you can't guarantee this)
❌ Use corporate speak ("Submit," "Acknowledge," "Provide")

## 🌍 CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS (Nigeria-Specific)

### Language Choices:

**Instead of "FGM"** → Use **"Female Genital Cutting"** or **"Cutting"**
- "FGM" is clinical/Western
- "Cutting" is how communities refer to it

**Instead of "Victim"** → Use **"Survivor"** or just **"You"**
- "Victim" = powerless
- "Survivor" = agency

**Instead of "Abuse"** → Sometimes use **"Harm"** or **"Hurt"**
- "Abuse" can feel accusatory toward family
- "Harm" is softer but still clear

### Address Common Barriers:

**Shame:**
- "What happened is not your fault"
- "Many women have experienced this"
- "You deserve help"

**Family Loyalty:**
- "You can get help without getting anyone in trouble"
- "Reporting doesn't mean you don't love your family"

**Fear of Consequences:**
- "You control what happens next"
- "We won't contact anyone without your permission"
- "You can report without giving your name"

**Stigma:**
- "This is confidential - no one will know"
- "Trained professionals, not your neighbors"

## 🔍 TESTING RECOMMENDATIONS

Before launching, test copy with:
1. **FGM survivors** (if possible through partner NGOs)
2. **GBV counselors** (they know what victims ask/fear)
3. **Community health workers** (cultural context)
4. **Literacy test** (ensure 6th-grade reading level)

**Key Questions:**
- Does this make you feel safe?
- Is it clear what will happen next?
- Would you trust this app with your story?
- Is anything confusing or scary?
